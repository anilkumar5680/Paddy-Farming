step3:planting

When choosing the suitable planting method, the (1) locality, (2) type of soil, (3) rice ecosystem, and (4) availability of inputs and labor, should be considered. Choosing when to is crucial to establishing the crop in the field. Timely planting into a well prepared seedbed will help produce a fast growing, uniform crop that will have higher yields and better competition against weeds and other pests. The best time to plant depends on locality, variety, weather, water availability, and the best harvest time. Planting at the same time (or within a 2 week window) as the neighboring fields can help to minimize insect, disease, bird, and rat pressure on individual fields.

step4:water management :

Cultivated rice is extremely sensitive to water shortages and when the soil water content drops below saturation, most rice varieties develop symptoms of water stress Good water management practices are needed to keep usage at optimum levels and to maximize rice yield.
